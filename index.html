About Me

I am a Junior at Northwestern University studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Science, advised by Prof. Doug Downey. My current research focuses on methods of evaluating and improving models for Natural Language Processing.

My academic interests include: machine learning, signal processing, computer vision and natural language processing. I’m particularly excited by the generation of new information from unstructured data.

Experience

> Research Assistant, Northwestern EECS January 2017 - Present

Principal Investigator: Prof. Doug Downey
I developed and co-authored a paper on the VecShare framework for sharing word embeddings online. This work was published in the Proceedings of EMNLP 2017 in Copenhagen, Denmark.

I am currently investigating active learning as a tool for improving deep statistical language models.
