About Me

I am a Junior at Northwestern University studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Science, advised by Prof. Doug Downey. My current research focuses on methods of evaluating and improving models for Natural Language Processing.

My research interests include machine learning and many of its applications: natural language processing, computer vision, and image processing. I’m particularly excited by the generation of new knowledge from unstructured data.

Experience

> Research Assistant, Northwestern EECS January 2017 - Present

Principal Investigator: Prof. Doug Downey
I developed and co-authored a paper on the VecShare framework for sharing word embedding NLP models over the internet. I am very excited to announce that I will be presenting this work at the upcoming EMNLP 2017 conference in Copenhagen, Denmark!

I am currently investigating how the inclusion of corpus-level statistics (e.g. unigram & bigram frequencies) can improve existing NLP models.
